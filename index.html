Key Sunsets Under the JCPOA and UNSC Resolution 2231

By Behnam Ben Taleblu and Andrea Stricker

A re-entry into the 2015 Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) by the United States would be a disservice to America’s nonproliferation objectives, as well as to President Joe Biden’s nascent Iran policy. Many of the major limitations included in the JCPOA and its accompanying United Nations Security Council (UNSC) resolution (2231) on the Islamic Republic’s nuclear and missile programs are set to expire or “sunset,” meaning Washington would be re-joining an accord that doesn’t block Iran’s pathways to nuclear weapons. After 2031, there are no provisions that stop Iran from producing and amassing weapon-grade uranium.
